# Glossary

**3:12 rules** (fåmansföretagsreglerna)
Swedish tax rules under IL 56-57 kap. governing the taxation of dividends and capital gains
from closely held companies (fåmansföretag). Income above a computed threshold (gränsbeloppet)
is taxed as labour income rather than capital income.

**arbetsgivaravgifter**
Swedish employer social-security contributions levied on gross wages. Rate schedule set
annually by government bill.

**attenuation bias**
Downward bias in an OLS regression coefficient caused by measurement error in the
right-hand-side variable. In the IGE context, annual income is a noisy proxy for
permanent income, attenuating the estimated elasticity toward zero.

**Atkinson index**
Inequality measure that explicitly incorporates a social inequality-aversion parameter ε;
higher ε implies greater weight on the lower tail of the income distribution.

**bostadsbidrag / bostadstillägg**
Swedish housing allowance programmes; bostadsbidrag targets families with children and
young adults, bostadstillägg targets pensioners.

**brytpunkt**
Statutory threshold at which Swedish personal income tax switches from the municipal rate
alone to the municipal rate plus the state surcharge (statlig inkomstskatt). Key behavioural
focal point in the ETI literature.

**bunching**
Excess mass of taxpayers observed just below a kink or notch in the tax schedule.
Bunching estimators identify the elasticity of taxable income from the size of this excess
mass relative to a counterfactual density.

**Callaway-Sant'Anna**
Difference-in-differences estimator (Callaway and Sant'Anna, 2021) robust to staggered
treatment adoption and heterogeneous treatment timing.

**comprehensive income taxation**
The 1991 Swedish tax reform ideal: all income—labour, capital, business—taxed at a
unified rate schedule. Partially abandoned through the dual income tax system.

**de Chaisemartin-D'Haultfoeuille**
DiD estimator (de Chaisemartin and D'Haultfoeuille, 2020) that allows treatment effect
heterogeneity across cohorts and periods under staggered adoption.

**dual income tax**
Tax system that taxes capital income at a lower flat rate while labour income faces a
progressive schedule. Sweden adopted elements of this in 1991.

**egenavgifter**
Swedish self-employment social-security contributions; equivalent of employer+employee
contributions for sole traders.

**ETI** (elasticity of taxable income)
The percentage change in reported taxable income for a one-percent change in the net-of-tax
rate (1 − marginal rate). Intensive-margin ETI ≈ 0.3, extensive-margin ETI ≈ 0.1 in
Swedish estimates.

**extensive margin**
Decision of whether to participate at all in a taxable activity (labour supply, self-
employment). Relevant at low-income ranges around the earned-income tax credit phase-in.

**föräldrapenning**
Swedish parental leave benefit paid by Försäkringskassan; included in the transfer module
of the simulator.

**Gini coefficient**
Standard summary statistic of income inequality; equals twice the area between the Lorenz
curve and the 45-degree line of perfect equality.

**influence function**
The linear approximation to the change in a functional estimator from adding one observation;
used here to compute asymptotically valid standard errors for inequality indices without
resampling.

**intensive margin**
Adjustments to hours worked or taxable income conditional on participation, in response to
a change in the marginal tax rate.

**jobbskatteavdrag** (earned-income tax credit)
Swedish earned-income tax credit introduced in 2007; deducted from municipal tax liability,
phased in from zero earnings and phased out at high incomes since 2012.

**Kakwani index**
Measure of tax progressivity defined as the difference between the concentration index of
taxes and the Gini coefficient of pre-tax income. Positive value indicates progressive
taxation.

**kommunal inkomstskatt**
Swedish municipal income tax; a flat rate (typically 30–35 %) levied on all earned income
above a basic deduction, varying by municipality.

**residual progression**
Residual-income progression index measuring the proportional reduction in inequality
(Gini) caused by the tax system; equals one minus the ratio of post-tax to pre-tax Gini.

**sjukpenning**
Swedish sickness benefit paid by Försäkringskassan; included in the transfer module.

**statlig inkomstskatt**
Swedish state income tax surcharge of 20 % levied on earned income above the upper
brytpunkt and 5 % above the lower brytpunkt (2015 onwards).

**Suits index**
Progressivity measure analogous to the Kakwani index but based on the cumulative
income share rather than the Lorenz ordinate.

**Sun-Abraham**
Difference-in-differences estimator (Sun and Abraham, 2021) that recovers cohort-specific
average treatment effects under staggered adoption without the negative-weight problem.

**taxable income**
Total income subject to tax after statutory deductions; differs from gross income by the
general deduction (grundavdrag), pension contributions, etc.

**Theil index**
Entropy-based inequality measure; more sensitive to changes in the upper tail than the
Gini coefficient.

**two-way fixed-effects DiD**
Standard difference-in-differences estimator with unit and time fixed effects; may be
biased under staggered treatment and heterogeneous effects (Goodman-Bacon, 2021).

**arbetslöshetsersättning**
Swedish unemployment insurance benefit; included in the transfer module of the simulator.
